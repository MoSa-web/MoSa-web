Abstract

We introduce MoSa, a novel hierarchical motion generation framework for text-driven 3D human motion generation that enhances the Vector Quantization-guided Generative Transformers (VQ-GT) paradigm through a coarse-to-fine scalable generation process. In MoSa, we propose a multi-scale token preservation strategy integrated into an improved hierarchical residual vector quantization variational autoencoder (RQ-VAE), which helps preserve intermediate-scale motion tokens. Moreover, a scalable autoregressive (SAR) transformer is employed to process motion sequences progressively, ensuring consistency between encoding and generation. Unlike traditional autoregressive models that predict one token at a time, SAR supports parallel multi-token prediction at varying scales, improving inference efficiency. Extensive experiments demonstrate that MoSa achieves state-of-the-art motion fidelity while improving the inference speed by 27% over MoMask. In particular, on the Motion-X dataset, MoSa achieves an FID of 0.18 (versus 0.20 of MoMask), which demonstrates superior generative quality and efficiency.
